# Safety — OOW Exam Focus

Safety covers emergency procedures, fire-fighting, life-saving appliances, and man overboard recovery. Examiners expect structured responses showing you can take command of any emergency.

KEY EXAM AREAS:
- Fire triangle, fire classes (A-F), and fixed extinguishing systems (CO2, foam, water spray)
- Engine room fire procedure — boundary cooling, ventilation shutdown, release sequence
- LSA requirements — lifeboat capacity, launching procedure, HRU operation
- MOB recovery turns — Williamson (delayed response), Anderson (immediate), Scharnow (high speed)
- Muster list requirements and emergency signals — know the difference between EVERY signal

CRITICAL — EMERGENCY SIGNALS (examiners test this heavily, candidates get it wrong):
- General alarm: continuous ringing of alarm bell or 7 short + 1 long blast on whistle. This means GO TO YOUR MUSTER STATION. It does NOT mean abandon ship.
- Abandon ship: this is ONLY a verbal command given by the Master. There is NO dedicated alarm signal for abandon ship. The Master orders abandon ship verbally after assessing the situation. No one else can give this order.
- Fire alarm: continuous sounding of fire alarm bells. Crew go to fire stations per muster list.
- MOB alarm: 3 long blasts on whistle (not universally standardised, check vessel SMS)
- If a candidate says "general alarm means abandon ship" — that is WRONG and would concern an examiner

COMMON MISTAKES:
- CONFUSING GENERAL ALARM WITH ABANDON SHIP — this is a critical error. General alarm = muster stations. Abandon ship = verbal order from Master ONLY.
- Not mentioning boundary cooling before releasing CO2 in engine room fires
- Confusing lifeboat launching sequence steps
- Describing MOB turns without stating when each is most appropriate
- Not knowing HRU activation depth (1.5-4m) and servicing interval
- Not knowing that ONLY the Master can order abandon ship
